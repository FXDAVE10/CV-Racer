Beyond raw speed, successful racing requires a diverse skill set. Here are the core competencies that define my approach to motorsport.

Technical Knowledge

Physics, mechanics, and data analysis

Physical Conditioning

Strength, endurance, and reflexes

Team Collaboration

Communication and feedback

Physics Knowledge

I understand how grip, braking, acceleration and weight transfer work in corners. I know why the kart behaves differently depending on speed, racing line and track conditions. I use basic physics to choose faster lines and lose as little time as possible.

Track Experience

I have many hours of track time and I adapt quickly to different circuits. I recognize good braking points, where I can push earlier and where I need to be patient. Track experience taught me to stay calm under pressure and adjust my driving style.

Hard Physical Training

I train regularly for strength, endurance and cardio. I work a lot on neck, arms and core to control the kart in long sessions. I also train my reflexes because in racing every millisecond matters.

Daily Discipline

I follow a daily routine with physical training, race analysis and learning time. I organize my schedule to balance school and karting. Discipline helps me stay consistent and keep improving even on hard days.

Data Analysis & Telemetry

I analyze telemetry data like lap times, corner speed, braking and acceleration. I compare my laps to see where I lose time and what I can improve. Data helps me drive smarter, not just harder.

Teamwork & Collaboration

I work well with mechanics, coaches and teammates. I listen to feedback and communicate clearly what I feel in the kart. Good collaboration helps the whole team improve and perform better on track.
